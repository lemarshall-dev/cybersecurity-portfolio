# TryHackMe Careers in cyber Write-up

## Objective

Understand what sort of careers are available in the cybersecurity field.

## Tools Used

-TryHackMe platform

## What I did

- Used the TryHackMe platform to research and understand what careers are available and begin to think about which role I'd like to steer towards.
- Completed the end of room quiz to determine which role would fit me most. After thinking about a few questions, they determined a penetration tester would fit me best.

## What I learned

- There's a high demand for cyber roles, over 3.5 million unfilled roles
- Constantly evolving field, excellent for those who thrive on learning new things
### Security Analyst
- Security analysts are often reffered to as digital defenders, they sit on the blue team. Their role is to investigate and piece together potential security issues and decide if further action is needed.
- You can move into areas like threat hunting, incident response, or malware analysis
### Security Engineer
- Security Engineers build and maintain the systems and processes that protect an organisation's network and devices
- Intrusion Detection System (IDS), which can be considered a security camera within an organisation's digital environment.
- Assessing risk and making sure systems and applications are protected against vulnerabilities is a major part of their day
### Penetration Tester (Pen Test)
- A penetration tester’s job is to check how secure a company’s systems and software are by safely trying to break into them.
- They search for weaknesses and vulnerabilities and then test if those can be exploited
- Everything is done under an agreement between the company and the pentester. This process is known as an engagement.
- Red teaming is where you simulate full-scale, long-term attacks across an entire organisation, sometimes even involving physical intrusion.
